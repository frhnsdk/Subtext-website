10 Ways to Show App Screenshots

Current: phone-frame carousel with side peeks. Pick ideas you like — can select multiple.

1

Floating Devices — Angled 3D

Screenshots rendered at perspective/tilt on phone mockups. 2–3 phones overlapping at different depths. Feels premium, like Apple landing pages.

2

Scroll-Driven Reveal

As user scrolls, each screenshot slides or fades in from the side, one at a time. No carousel needed — scrolling IS the navigation.

3

Feature + Screenshot Split

Left: feature name + description. Right: matching screenshot. Alternates left/right as you scroll (like Stripe's product pages).

4

Auto-Playing Slideshow

Screenshots cycle automatically every 3–4s inside a centered phone frame. Subtle crossfade. No user interaction needed — passive showcase.

5

Horizontal Scroll Marquee

Screenshots in phone frames scroll infinitely left, like a ticker. Looping, smooth, no interaction. Works great as a section break.

6

Interactive Click-to-Zoom

Thumbnail grid of all screenshots. Click any → expands fullscreen with a dark overlay. Arrow keys to navigate. Clean lightbox feel.

7

Tabbed Feature Switcher

Tabs across the top: "Chats", "Profile", "Settings", etc. Clicking a tab swaps the screenshot. Ties visuals directly to features.

8

Stacked Deck / Fan Layout

Screenshots stacked like playing cards, fanned out. Hover/click peels top card to reveal the next. Tactile, playful, memorable.

9

Mockup Inside MacBook + Phone

Show phone screenshot inside a realistic phone mockup SVG — but also pair it with a desktop/tablet mockup if a web version exists. Adds context.

10

Parallax Floating Screenshots

Screenshots float at different z-depths. Mouse movement causes subtle parallax shift — foreground moves more, background less. Immersive hero feel.
